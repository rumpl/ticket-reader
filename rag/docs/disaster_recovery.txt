# Acme Corp — Disaster Recovery & Business Continuity

## DR Strategy (DR-001)
**Last Updated:** February 2025 | **RPO:** 1 hour | **RTO:** 4 hours

### Recovery Tiers

| Tier | Systems | RPO | RTO | Strategy |
|------|---------|-----|-----|----------|
| Tier 1 (Critical) | API Gateway, Auth, Payments, Core DB | 15 min | 1 hour | Hot standby in DR region |
| Tier 2 (Important) | Analytics, Search, Notifications | 1 hour | 4 hours | Warm standby |
| Tier 3 (Standard) | Internal tools, CI/CD, Dev environments | 24 hours | 48 hours | Cold backup, rebuild from IaC |

### Backup Strategy
- **Aurora PostgreSQL**: Continuous replication to DR region, point-in-time recovery
- **Redis**: Snapshot every 15 minutes, replicated cross-region
- **Kafka**: MirrorMaker 2 replicates critical topics to DR cluster
- **S3**: Cross-region replication enabled for all production buckets
- **Secrets (Vault)**: Active-active replication across regions
- **Infrastructure-as-Code**: All infra defined in Terraform, stored in GitHub
  (can rebuild any environment from scratch in <2 hours)

### Failover Procedures

#### Automated Failover
- Route53 health checks trigger DNS failover for API endpoints
- Aurora automatically promotes read replica in DR region
- Kubernetes workloads rescheduled via cluster federation

#### Manual Failover (Requires On-Call + SRE Lead)
1. Confirm primary region is unrecoverable (>15 min outage of Tier 1 services)
2. Notify incident commander and leadership via PagerDuty escalation
3. Execute `make failover-dr` from the infra-ops repository
4. Verify data integrity checks pass (automated suite)
5. Update status page (status.acmecorp.com)
6. Monitor for 2 hours before declaring stable

### DR Testing
- **Monthly**: Automated backup restoration tests (all tiers)
- **Quarterly**: Tier 2/3 failover exercise
- **Semi-annually**: Full DR failover drill (Tier 1 included, 4-hour window on Saturday)
- **Annually**: Chaos engineering game day (random failure injection in production)

Last full DR test: January 2025 — Completed successfully in 3 hours 12 minutes.
Issue found: Kafka MirrorMaker lag exceeded 5 minutes under load. Fixed by
increasing consumer parallelism (see POST-MORTEM-2025-003).
