# Acme Corp — IT Security Policies

## Password Policy (POL-SEC-001)
**Effective Date:** January 2025
**Review Cycle:** Annual

### Requirements
- Minimum 14 characters
- Must include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
- Passwords expire every 90 days
- Last 12 passwords cannot be reused
- Account locks after 5 failed attempts for 30 minutes

### Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
MFA is **mandatory** for all employees accessing:
- Corporate email
- VPN and remote access
- Cloud platforms (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- Source code repositories
- HR and financial systems

Approved MFA methods: hardware security keys (preferred), authenticator apps.
SMS-based MFA is **not permitted** since Q3 2024.

### Service Accounts
- Must use API keys or certificates, never passwords
- Rotated every 60 days
- Require approval from the Security team via ServiceNow ticket (category: SEC-ACCESS)

## Data Classification (POL-SEC-002)

All data must be classified into one of four levels:

| Level | Label | Examples | Handling |
|-------|-------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Public | Marketing materials, blog posts | No restrictions |
| 2 | Internal | Org charts, internal memos | Do not share externally |
| 3 | Confidential | Customer data, financials | Encrypted at rest and in transit |
| 4 | Restricted | PII, credentials, M&A data | Need-to-know basis, audit trail required |

## Incident Response (POL-SEC-003)

1. **Report** — Immediately notify #security-incidents on Slack
2. **Triage** — Security on-call classifies severity (P1-P4) within 15 minutes
3. **Contain** — Isolate affected systems
4. **Investigate** — Root cause analysis within 48 hours
5. **Remediate** — Fix and verify
6. **Post-mortem** — Blameless review within 5 business days

**P1 incidents** (data breach, ransomware) require immediate escalation to the CISO
and legal team. Customers must be notified within 72 hours per GDPR/CCPA.
